NingBGM: Multi-Agent Collaborative Music Generation for Comprehensive Scene Understanding

Abstract

Existing music generation methods are mostly driven by a single input modality, making it difficult to fully capture and exploit the rich multimodal information in real-world scenarios; as a result, the generated music is often insufficiently aligned with the scene in both semantics and emotion. To address this, we propose NingBGM, a multi-agent background-music generation framework for comprehensive scene understanding. The framework aims to drive high-quality, scene-adaptive music generation by deeply and structurally understanding complete scenes composed of video, audio, images, and text. First, we build CSD-200, the first dataset for this task, containing 200 samples with fine-grained structured annotations for each modality. Second, we design a supervisor-based hierarchical multi-agent collaboration architecture. Through an \"Agent Profile\" mechanism, the framework instantiates general large language models as domain experts and coordinates three agent teams?requirement analysis, scene understanding, and music generation?to simulate a professional creative workflow of \"plan?execute?verify\", enabling end-to-end music generation. Experimental results show that NingBGM outperforms existing baseline models on AudioBox music-quality metrics, an improved CLAP scene-matching score, and subjective/objective MOS evaluations; on some metrics, it even surpasses real-world music samples. Ablation studies further verify the necessity of multimodal fusion?especially the video modality?for improving scene?music alignment. This study demonstrates the effectiveness of a multi-agent collaborative paradigm based on comprehensive scene understanding for music generation.
